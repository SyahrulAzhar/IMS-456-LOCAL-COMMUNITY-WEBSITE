History of Jenderam Hilir


Jenderam Hilir is an area that is located in Sepang District, Selangor near to the Langat River, which became the main route for traders and migrants froma Sumatera, Javanese, and China to Kajang, Semenyih, Reko, and many more.

Jenderam Port

In 1940, an acient oar named "Bot Jenderam" was found. Also, pieces of ancient potteries were also found Those potteris were decorated using carved paddle-impressed method. Those ancient artifacts were found in or near the Jenderam Port

In 1977, tin ore miner company, Syarikat Tit Nam Hoong found more artifacts in the port. Those artifacts include ancient foreign potteries, Chinese ceramics and potteries, few metal objects such as tin ingots and bronze containers, and few wooden tools such as wooden oars and ston pestles. All of the artifacts then were exhibited in Muzium Shah Alam, Selangor.

Malay Communists

During emergency era of 1948 to 1960, many of the Malay villagers supported and become follower of Parti Komunis Malaya (PKM). This is because many of the Indonesian communist leaders such as Tan Malaka was believed to came to Jenderam Hilir as there are many locals are from Minagkabau descent.

One Malay communis leader named Abdul Manan have invited few of Kampung Sungai Sembung, Semenyih villagers to Jenderam to discuss the communists crusade against British colonialism and the liberation of the Malay as well as further activities.
